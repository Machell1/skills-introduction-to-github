9.1 Appreciating Customer's Report

When a member of the public makes a report of a crime or incident, the police shall:

  1. Greet the customer per the JCF Police Public Interaction Policy (JCF/FW/PL/Gen/0002/2021)
  2. Inform supervisor, who shall establish whether investigation is by uniformed or non-uniformed personnel (including C-TOC, CISOCA etc.) per Appendix 5
  3. Take appropriate action or refer for required investigator
  4. Where non-uniformed investigators unavailable, refer to supervisor for preliminary investigation by uniformed personnel
  5. Record statement and complete appropriate CR forms
  6. Record entry in Crime Diary or Station Diary/SRMS and relevant registers
  7. Where applicable, send email/radio message in timely manner
  8. Complete and issue Customer Reference Form (CR 10) to complainant
  9. Complete Stolen Property Form (CR 4) in triplicate where property is reported stolen/recovered
NB: A customer should not be referred to another station to make a report, but be promptly attended to per the JCF Police Public Interaction Policy.
9.1.10 Recording of Witness or Complainant Statement
  • Every effort shall be made to record a statement in the initial stage
  • Record a detailed, chronological account of events
  • For all major crimes, a Witness Bio-Data Form (CR 3) must be completed
  • Complete and attach Investigator's Worksheet (CR 1) and Action Sheet (CR 2)

9.1.13 Case Reference Number

Each case file will be assigned a unique Case Reference Number (CR#). The format for DCRR files:

{DCRR consecutive#}_{yyyy/mm/dd}/{station initial}/{SD or CD}{entry#}/{division code}

Example 1: 32_2014/03/05/LT/SD45/M (Lionel Town, Station Diary #45, Manchester)

Example 2: 43_2017/06/10/HWT/CD52/SAC (Half Way Tree, Crime Diary #52, St. Andrew Central)

9.2 Case Assignment
9.2.1 Preliminary Vetting
  • Registrar and Station Manager shall review contents and ensure all CR forms are completed
  • Cases must be properly classified (uniformed or non-uniformed)
  • Tasks shall be generated on the Action Sheet (CR 2) with specific timelines
9.2.2 Assignment Factors

When assigning cases, the Registrar or Station Manager shall consider:

  • Competence of the intended investigator
  • Expertise relevant to the crime type
  • Availability of the investigator
  • Workload of the intended investigator

The Divisional Commander or Crime Officer reserves the right to make the final assignment decision.

9.3 Management of Cases and Investigations
9.3.2 Morning Crime Report (CR 7)
  • Summary of all major and minor crimes over a 24-hour period (5:30am to 5:30am)
  • Prepared and sent to SIMU via statistics@jcf.gov.jm and copied to CIB HQ via cmu.cibhq@jcf.gov.jm by 6:00am daily
  • Information protected under the Data Protection Act, 2020 - shall not be transmitted via unauthorized medium

9.3.4 Interviews and Confessions
  • Questioning of suspects recorded using Q&A Suspect Form (CR 8)
  • Questioning of accused recorded using Q&A Accused Form (CR 9)
  • Confessions recorded using Confession Statement Form (CR 11)
  • Where practicable, confessions shall be audio-visually recorded
  • All confessions shall be investigated to determine truthfulness
9.3.6 Submission of Cases and Court Attendance
  • Case files submitted to DCR with first responding and arresting officers' statements
  • DCR should receive file at least 48 hours prior to court deadline
  • Major and minor crimes submitted with Major and Minor Case Report Form (CR 12)
  • Case files submitted with Court Case File Checklist (CR 13)
  • JCF Profile Form (CR 14) completed for ALL case files to court
  • Application for Remand or Bail Conditions (CR 15) where applicable
9.3.7 Case Closure

Per Section 6.3.4, a case may be closed when circumstances are satisfied. The Registrar or Divisional Crime Officer within the Division are empowered to declare a case closed. ACP CIB, Director of Investigations CIB HQ and Area Crime Officer may also declare a case closed.

All cases declared closed shall be recorded in RED on the Major and Minor Case Report Form (CR 12).

9.3.9 Case Suspended

When all investigative leads have been exhausted, the IO submits a written report recommending suspension. Suspended cases shall be reviewed every 90 days.

9.3.10 Cold Case

A Divisional Crime Officer may deem a case cold after it has been suspended for 3 years. Cold cases may be reviewed when new information is unearthed, technology advances, or it serves the public interest.

9.3.11 Disposition of Case Reports

Case files closed for a continuous period of 7 years may be disposed of per the JCF Records Information Management Policy. ACP Administration Branch has overall responsibility for destruction of files.
