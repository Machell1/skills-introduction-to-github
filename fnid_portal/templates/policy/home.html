JCF Case Management Policy & Standard Operating Procedures

JCF/FW/PL/C&S/0001/2024
1.0 Introduction

In our continued effort to advance the transformation of the Jamaica Constabulary Force (JCF), the organization has reviewed its crime reporting and case management processes in order to more effectively manage the increasingly changing criminal landscape.

This document provides the policy framework as well as the required procedures for the management of crime and other 'incidents'. More specifically, it provides general guidance and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for the appreciation, assignment, investigation and closure of cases.

This Case Management Policy and Standard Operating Procedures create the foundation for the establishment and maintenance of a more effective and efficient system of managing crime reports and investigations, while ensuring proper accountability and adherence to industry standards.

2.0 Purpose

The purpose of this Policy and Standard Operating Procedures is to outline the process for the appreciation of crime/incident reports and the management of related investigations.

3.0 Scope

This Policy and Standard Operating Procedures applies to all sworn and unsworn members of the JCF and it covers the management of all crime/incident reports and related investigations.

4.0 Policy Statement

It is the policy of the JCF that:

  1. 4.1 All reports of crime and other incidents will be recorded promptly and accurately;
  2. 4.2 Investigations will be thorough, transparent, confidential, honest and conducted with respect for human rights and dignity;
  3. 4.3 Complainants, victims and witnesses will be kept informed of the status of the investigation;
  4. 4.4 Only authorized persons will be permitted to process information and personal data of complainants, witnesses, victims, suspects, jurors or others; and
  5. 4.5 All efforts will be made to bring closure to cases in a timely manner.

Case Status Categories (Section 6.3)
Case Open (6.3.1)

A case that is actively under investigation.

Case Suspended (6.3.2)

A case that has been put on hold after all investigative leads have been exhausted.

Case Cleared (6.3.3)

A case is considered cleared when charge(s) have been laid, suspect is dead, warrant issued (life sentence abroad), extradition denied, victim will not prosecute, submitted to Coroners Court, cleared by Child Diversion, suspect summoned, or report was contrived.

Case Closed (6.3.4)

A case is considered closed when charge(s) have been preferred and no other suspect is being pursued, suspect is dead and no other suspect is being pursued, or any other circumstances ACP CIB may deem necessary.

Cold Case (6.3.5)

A reported crime or incident under investigation that has remained unsolved and inactive for a period of three years after it was declared suspended.
